## Head-to-Head Comparison: Key Categories

**Empower** provides budget tracking within its broader platform, but it's not the primary focus. You can set spending limits and track categories, though the interface feels like one tool among many.

**Mint** wins decisively here. It's specifically built for budgeting, with intuitive category management, customizable spending limits, and clear visualizations that help you understand where money goes monthly.

**Winner: Mint** — if budgeting is your primary need.

### Investment Management and Wealth Building

**Empower** dominates this category with integrated investment accounts, robo-advisory services, and portfolio management. By 2026, Empower users can manage multiple investment accounts, receive personalized recommendations, and even access tax-loss harvesting features.

**Winner: Empower** — by a significant margin.

**Empower** includes sophisticated retirement calculators that project whether you're on track, account for Social Security, and suggest adjustments. The platform integrates with all your retirement accounts for a complete picture.

**Winner: Empower** — clearly.

Both platforms offered solid mobile experiences as of 2026.

**Winner: Mint** — for budget-conscious users who don't need advanced features.

## FAQ

### Can I use both Empower and Mint together?

Yes, many users run both simultaneously. Mint for pure budgeting, Empower for investment tracking and planning. However, you'll have some redundancy since both track spending. Most find using one or the other sufficient.

### Is Empower worth the monthly cost compared to free Mint?

If you have investments and want professional guidance, absolutely. The robo-advisory fees and tax-loss harvesting features often save money compared to traditional financial advisors. If you're budgeting-only with no investments, stick with Mint.

### Which app is better for beginners in 2026?

**Mint** is unquestionably better for beginners. It's free, simple, and teaches you the fundamentals of budgeting without overwhelming options. Once you have investments to manage, consider upgrading to Empower.
