## Comparison: Shared vs. Managed WordPress Hosting

### Shared Hosting (SiteGround, Hostinger, Bluehost)
**Pros:**
- Affordable entry-level pricing
- Suitable for small to medium sites
- Easy to manage multiple sites
- No technical knowledge required

**Cons:**
- Shared server resources
- Performance affected by other sites
- Limited optimization
- Basic support sometimes

### Managed WordPress Hosting (WP Engine, Kinsta)
**Pros:**
- Expert WordPress optimization
- Better performance and security
- Dedicated support
- Automatic updates and backups
- Scalable for growth

**Cons:**
- Higher monthly costs
- WordPress-only (less flexibility)
- Over-engineered for small sites

## FAQ

### Q: Is shared hosting sufficient for WordPress in 2025?

A: Yes, shared hosting works well for WordPress sites with moderate traffic (under 100k monthly visitors). Services like SiteGround and Hostinger offer WordPress-optimized shared hosting with sufficient performance. However, if you run multiple WordPress sites professionally, managed WordPress hosting provides better scalability and support.

### Q: How much should I expect to spend on web hosting?

A: Reliable hosting ranges from $2-20/month for small projects to $35+ for premium managed services. Budget $3-10/month for quality shared hosting, $15-30/month for managed WordPress, and $50+ for enterprise solutions. Remember: cheap hosting often leads to performance problems and support frustrations.

### Q: Can I switch hosting providers if I'm unhappy?

A: Yes, most quality hosts offer money-back guarantees (30-45 days) and provide free migration assistance. However, switching involves some downtime risk, so choose carefully initially. SiteGround and WP Engine both offer free migrations from competitors.
